Design and production
  • Hiroyuki Watanabe(1996~2001)
Wood
  • Japanese native lacquer - natural wood
  • 100 year old lacquer tree from Futatsui, Akita Prefecture
  • Garden Kew Quin collection, a map of the distribution of lacquer trees recorded in the report on the Japanese lacquer industry by the British Consulate in Hakodate (1882), showing the trees growing in the natural wood areas.
  • Joboji Cultural Hall Log cut where it meets the ceiling.
  • Carved with a machete. Inside, finished only with a circular chisel.
Sap of the middle coat
  • Raw lacquer used for the Joboji Cultural Hall monument and the Japan Urushi Scratching Technique Preservation Society commission work
