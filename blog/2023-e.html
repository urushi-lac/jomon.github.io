The history of kanshitu

2023-09-23T17:30
This coffin was excavated from the Han tomb of Ma Wangdui .(wikipedia)
Many burial accessories made from 夾紵胎 have been unearthed in Han dynasty (BC200) tomb burial groups. 夾紵胎 is a so-called kanshitu , made by pasting layers of linen and silk together to form a base.
The technique had been established in China during this period. During the same period, kanshitu products were also found at a site in Lelang on the Korean Peninsula. Although there are earthenware and wood-based lacquered products, no earlier kanshitu products have been excavated.
The oldest known kanshitu products of world from Hokkaido in 7000 BC. The oldest Chinese lacquer-products from 5000 BC, the oldest Korean lacquer-products from 3000 BC. The oldest Chinese and Korean kanshitu products from 200 BC.
It is very significant that the kanshitu technique currently originated in Japan and was used for burials.

Vermilion lacquer bowl

2023-08-30T13:00
Vermilion lacquer bowl, Tokyo National Museum collection. 1391
The inside and outside colour is different.
The black outer part is an oil film that has adhered to the vermilion lacquering. Even if it is wiped off well with a soft cloth after use, the oil remains and after a long time it will appear black. Even if the finish is even, the dents in the annual rings and areas that are frequently touched will cause changes in the landscape. The mouth rim has been used so much that the vermilion lacquer has worn off and appears black. This is not dirt. It is not known what caused the crack in the centre. Looking at the whole piece, you get a sense of something like the character of the age it has undergone.
The inside appears to have been restored. If the type of lacquer sap and pigment is not the same as it was in the past, it will not blend in with the colour of the outside after continued use. Original species lacquer trees are suitable for restoring vessels. Restoration of vermilion lacquer is a difficult task and there is room for research.

Vegetation

2023-06-06T17:40
There are White Birch trees. And Sasa kurilensis grows in tufts under mountain forests.
White Birch is a broad-leaved deciduous tree that grows in snowy areas.
Sasa kurilensis (Nemagaridake) is 1-3 m tall and is characterised by its roots, which are said to bend under the weight of snow in areas with heavy snowfall. It often forms large colonies that fill mountain slopes.
In Joboji, the criteria for selecting a site for planting Japanese lacquer trees is that it must be lower than where the White Birch trees grow in clusters. After the Japanese lacquer trees is planted, the undergrowth is cleared until the saplings are higher than the Sasa kurilensis, after which the Japanese lacquer trees grow without any further work.
It must be natural that Japanese lacquer trees have been found here and there in the north-east for a long time!

Lacquer seeds and Paper wasp

2023-03-25T15:00
The photograph shows hemp, lacquer seeds and an paper wasp together with my urn in my studio.
Lacquer and Paper wasps are each thought to have emerged more than 100 million years ago.
The angiosperms to which the lacquer tree belongs are protected from the outside environment by their seeds, allowing them to survive in harsh conditions such as dryness and low temperatures. Having flowers also plays a role in attracting insects and other animals, improving the pollination process and facilitating plant reproduction.
On the other hand, it has been found that the paper wasp built its nests from sap and plant fibres, which, when the sap is lacquer, is the origin of dry lacquer.
Through this information, we can learn about the relationships between organisms in nature, which gives us an insight into how organisms are used. It also provides a deeper understanding of the diversity of life and its adaptability to the environment during the process of evolution.
